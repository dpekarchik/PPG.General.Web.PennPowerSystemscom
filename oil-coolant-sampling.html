Suggested timing

What we sample — and how often.

Oil sampling

The lubrication system cools and lubricates your generator engine and equipment.
Recommended: every 250 hours or 12 months, whichever comes first.

Coolant sampling

Changes in coolant quality or depletion of essential elements reduce protection for your engine's coolant system.
Recommended: every 12 months.

Fuel sampling

Lab testing detects water and microbial contamination that can keep your generator from starting when it's needed most.
Recommended: every 12 months.

When to sample

Timing depends on your equipment.

The right interval depends on the generator type, operating conditions, and manufacturer recommendations. A few general guidelines:

  • Newly commissioned equipment — establish a baseline shortly after commissioning for future comparisons.
  • Regular maintenance intervals — routine analysis surfaces adverse conditions early — typically every 3, 6, or 12 months depending on type and usage.
  • Critical operating conditions — sample more frequently under unusual loads, high temperatures, or other stress.
  • After significant events — sample following outages, overheating incidents, or other abnormal conditions.
  • Changes in operating conditions — adjust frequency when load increases or run hours extend.
  • Condition monitoring systems — some units trigger sampling automatically based on continuous analysis.

Routine oil analysis identifies adverse conditions in the lube oil that affect performance — protecting your machine's operation and lifespan. Contact us to set up a sampling program tailored to your equipment.
